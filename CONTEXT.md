# Meal Planner 3000 — Domain Context

## Glossary

### Household

The owning entity for all data in the system — Meals, Schedules, and Preferences. A Household is created on sign-up. Currently one Household maps to one User (the app owner). Multi-member households are a future concern.

### Meal

A known recipe belonging to a Household's Meal Pool. A Meal has a name, a Category, and metadata (diet, season, producesLeftovers, suitableFor, tags) and a list of Ingredients. Diet is one of three values: `meat`, `fish`, or `vegetarian`.

### Meal Suitability

A property on a Meal (`suitableFor`) indicating which meal times it is appropriate to **freshly cook**: `lunch`, `dinner`, or `any`. The Scheduler only places a Meal as a **Filled Slot** whose meal time matches the Meal's suitability. Suitability does not restrict Leftover Slots — leftovers of any Meal are eligible for any available Slot (lunch or dinner), since eating leftovers is not the same as freshly preparing the Meal. The default value for existing Meals is `any`.

### Tag

A free-form label on a Meal used for filtering and searching the Meal Pool (e.g. "quick", "batch cook", "date night"). Tags have no effect on Scheduler logic.

### Category

A user-defined label for classifying Meals by cuisine or format (e.g. pasta, curry, soup, stir-fry). Categories are managed by the Household and are distinct from free-form Tags. Each Meal belongs to one Category.

### Season

A property on a Meal indicating when it is appropriate to serve. The active season is determined automatically from the calendar date using fixed boundaries: Spring/Summer (April–September), Autumn/Winter (October–March), Festive (December). BBQ is a special case excluded from automatic season detection — to be developed further. Year-round Meals are always eligible regardless of season.

### Schedule

A plan for a Household assigning Meals to Slots over a 1, 2, or 4-week period. Generated by the Scheduler, then manually editable. A Household has one **active** Schedule and retains the immediately previous Schedule for use by the Scheduler (to avoid repeating recently served Meals). Schedules beyond the previous one are discarded.

### Slot

A single cell in a Schedule — identified by a date and a meal time (Lunch or Dinner). A Slot may be:

- **Filled** — assigned a Meal (freshly cooked)
- **Leftover** — references another Slot's Meal (the source), indicating the household eats the leftovers rather than cooking again
- **Empty** — intentionally skipped

### Household Preferences

Global settings owned by a Household that govern Schedule generation. Can be overridden per Schedule. Includes Scheduler Constraints (quotas) and Slot Configuration (which days and meal times are active by default).

### Slot Configuration

A per-day-of-week toggle for each meal time (Lunch, Dinner), defining which slots the Scheduler should fill by default. Stored as part of Household Preferences. Specific dates can be marked Empty after a Schedule is generated.

### Scheduler Constraints

Quota-based rules applied when generating a Schedule to ensure variety. Stored as global Household Preferences and can be overridden per Schedule. Current fixed constraints:

- **maxLeftoverMeals** — maximum number of Leftover Slots in the Schedule
- **maxMeatMeals** — maximum number of meat-diet Meals
- **maxFishMeals** — maximum number of fish-diet Meals
- **noPreviousScheduleRepeats** — Meals that appeared in the previous Schedule are excluded from selection

### Scheduling Rule

A user-defined selection constraint applied to every generated Schedule. Replaces the fixed `maxMeatMeals` and `maxFishMeals` constraints. Each Rule has:

- **Subject** — a Category, a Tag, or a Diet value (`meat`, `fish`, or `vegetarian`)
- **Operator** — `at most` or `at least` (best-effort — the Scheduler tries to honour `at least N` rules but silently undershoots rather than blocking generation if the Meal Pool is too small)
- **Value** — a non-negative integer
- **Scope** — `per_schedule` (default) or `per_day`. Controls the time window within which Value is counted. Only `at_most` Rules support `per_day` scope; `at_least` Rules are always `per_schedule`. A `per_day` Rule with value 1 and subject `diet:fish` prevents fish appearing at both lunch and dinner on the same calendar day. When only one slot per day is enabled in Slot Configuration, `per_day` and `per_schedule` behave identically — this is acceptable and requires no UI warning.

Rules are global — stored as a collection owned by the Household and applied to every generated Schedule. Multiple Rules per subject are allowed, enabling range constraints (e.g. "at most 4 AND at least 1 curry"). Category-based Rules are deleted automatically when the referenced Category is deleted (cascade).

The Rules UI warns (but does not block) when two Rules on the same subject are logically contradictory (e.g. "at most 1 curry" and "at least 3 curry").

On migration, existing `maxMeatMeals` and `maxFishMeals` values are converted to pre-populated Rules. Their per-schedule override fields (`maxMeatMealsOverride`, `maxFishMealsOverride`) are removed from the Schedule record and the generate form. `maxLeftoverMeals` remains a fixed setting (it controls leftover slot behaviour, not meal selection).

### Leftover

A Slot whose content is the leftovers of a Meal cooked in another Slot within the same Schedule. When the Scheduler assigns a Meal with `producesLeftovers: true` to a **dinner** Slot, it targets the next calendar day's **lunch** Slot first, then the next calendar day's **dinner** Slot. Falls back to the existing 1–2 position logic if neither next-day Slot is available. Leftover Slots are not constrained by the source Meal's `suitableFor` value — a dinner-only Meal can have its leftovers placed in a lunch Slot. The user can always swap the Leftover Slot manually after generation.

### Shopping List

A list of Ingredients aggregated across all filled (non-Leftover) Slots in a Schedule. Generated on demand from the active Schedule. Individual items can be checked off as the user shops or discovers they already have the item at home. Check-off state is per-Schedule only — there is no persistent pantry inventory.

### Ingredient

A structured item on a Meal — a name plus an optional quantity and unit (e.g. name: "chicken breast", quantity: 3, unit: null). Ingredients are stored per-Meal, not in a shared library. The structured form enables aggregation across a Schedule to produce a shopping list.
